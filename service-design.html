Service design is all about taking a step back, understanding a process or service as a whole, and trying to identify ways to improve it. At the Graduate College, the Information Services team wanted to reimagine the application and application review processes.

The first service design blueprint was an exploration in the overall process, beginning with a potential applicant being interested in the university and the work done by faculty over the summer for the new academic year, to the student’s first day of school and beyond.

THE FINAL PRODUCT

Available for download is the “Modernizing the Graduate Application Process” workbook. Outlined are the process and outcomes from the many service design workshops the Information Services team undertook to create a blueprint for the current application process and find solutions to common issues.

OVERARCHING PAIN POINTS

The 248 pain points were then grouped into eighteen (18) categories to help organize the issues in a more digestible manner. These groups were then given a priority, or importance, score based on how relevant and impactful they are to the application process. Every group that received a score greater than zero was included in the ideation phase, with these top six (6) given higher priority.

The remaining pain point groups, in order of importance from most to least, were as follows: General Tech Assistance, English Proficiency, Student Shenanigans, Program Requirements, Process Review Needed, and I-20 Process.

OVERARCHING OPPORTUNITIES

Overall, 5 areas of opportunity were discovered during the ideation stage across the entire service blueprint. An additional 6th area of opportunity was discovered; however, ideas focused on this area will be covered by the Information Services team.
